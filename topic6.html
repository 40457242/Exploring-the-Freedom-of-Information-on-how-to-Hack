Available at: https://abcnews.go.com/Technology/story?id=119423&page=1 [Accessed 7 May 2020].This is the news article describing an event mentioned in the audio clip. This page appears to be an accurate reflection of events coming from a reputable news site, it also quotes the US Justice Department so can be considered accurate as the report draws directly from the government body who’s actions it was reporting it. This makes it more likely the content of the report is accurate. On the other hand as a news website it has its own natural bias and agenda which may affect on its reporting however this article appears to offer no commentary on what it is reporting and instead seems to provide a detached view of the facts

Bennetto, J., 1997.Fine For Boy Who Hacked Into Pentagon. [online] The Independent. Available at: https://www.independent.co.uk/news/fine-for-boy-who-hacked-into-pentagon-1274204.html [Accessed 7 May 2020]This is another news article dated from 1997. It can be considered a decent article as it draws from the testimony of those involved and the statement of the magistrates. However this article appears to be less useful as it seems to heavy politicise and sensationalise the issue. The article seems to first offer up straight facts on the case before publishing statements from the US Senate that this boy was a serious threat to national security and seemingly giving voice to sensationalised words about the possible dangers instead of just reporting the facts. This and the poor reputation of the site suggest this article may need to be seen with healthy scepticism.

Her Majesty’s Government.2018. Cyber Security Breaches Survey 2018. [online] https://www.gov.uk/government/statistics/cyber-security-breaches-survey-2018 [Accessed 12/06/20]This source is produced and published by HMG as a result of theirstatistics for the year for this reason it be be considered accurate. They are not a news site with a political agenda, they are a government body releasing the facts so there is less danger of misrepresentation of information to fit an agenda. It could be considered that the agency may be trying to under-report crime figures to make themselves look more competent however this does not appear to be the case as some of the statistics contained within (particularly conviction rates) are very poor suggesting what is reported is accurate

Mark Bridge. 2018. Hackers go free from prosecution. [online]. The Times. https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/hackers-go-free-from-prosecution-drtld6ncp [Accessed 12/06/20]The times is a well known and well respected news site, whose reputation would suggest that te information they publish would be well researched and accurate. However they quote research down by an outside firm, making this secondary data. This could mean that data has been misinterpreted or that during the data’s collection the method may have not been perfect, because of this it can be considered t be a less useful source.
